# Century 4 (301-400 AD)

## Major Historical Events and Figures

- 306-337 AD **Constantine the Great** First Christian Roman Emperor #rulers [family]
- 312 AD **Battle of Milvian Bridge** Constantine defeats Maxentius, sees vision of cross #wars [family]
- 313 AD **Edict of Milan** Constantine legalizes Christianity throughout empire #worldhistory [family]
- 325 AD **Council of Nicaea** First ecumenical council addresses Arian controversy #worldhistory [family]
- 330 AD **Constantinople Founded** Constantine establishes "New Rome" in the East #construction [family]
- 361-363 AD **Julian the Apostate** Last pagan emperor attempts to restore old religion #rulers [family]
- 378 AD **Battle of Adrianople** Goths defeat Romans, Emperor Valens killed #wars [family]
- 379-395 AD **Theodosius I** Makes Christianity official religion of empire #rulers [family]
- 395 AD **Permanent Division** Empire split between East and West permanently #politics [family]

## Key Themes of This Century
- Triumph of Christianity under Constantine
- Foundation of Constantinople as eastern capital
- Barbarian invasions intensify pressure on frontiers
- Final division of Roman Empire
- Development of Christian theology and doctrine
- Decline of traditional Roman religion and culture
